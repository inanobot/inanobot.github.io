Giant Unilamellar Vesicle (GUV) Formation

14
Feb

Our dirty lab work...shh!

Last updated 13.10.16

Materials needed:
DOPC (25 mg/mL)
Biotin (25 mg/mL)
2:1 Chloro-methanol
DiI (25 mg/mL)
BSA (1 mg/mL)
Ethanol
Methanol
Chloroform
PBS
Chambers and Platinum caps
8-well Microscopy chamber

Procedure: Giant Unilamellar Vesicle (GUV) Preparation
1) Cleaning the Electroformation Vials
- Put the vials and Platinum caps into Ethanol and water (50%/50%) and sonicate for 20 minutes
- Rinse the chambers with Methanol, then Chloroform last. (Chloroform evaporate faster)
- Dry them completely

2) Lipid Mixture and Electroformation

               
IngredientsDOPC (25 mg/mL)Biotin (25 mg/mL) 2:1 Chloro-methanol DiI (25 mg/mL)
Volume (μL) 3.8 2 (1:10 dilution) 90 4

- Mix the lipids in an Eppendorf tube with the final total lipid concentration of 1 mg/mL. Since 5% of Biotin was used in the total lipid concentration, Biotin was diluted 1:10 using 2:1 Chloro-methanol as the solvent.
- Spread 5 μL of lipid mixture on different Platinum wires. Slowly pipet the lipid on the wires to make a nice even layer.
- Place the Platinum wires on top of the chambers and set it in a vacuum chamber to let chloroform evaporate completely from the wires for ~10 minutes
- Fill the chambers with 350 μL 300 mM sucrose solution
- Insert the Platinum wire into the chambers and seal the chambers with electrical tape
- Place the chambers in heat block and set the temperature to 68°C
- Start the alternating current: 10 Hertz, 2 volts for 2 hours
- Decrease the current to 2 Hertz for 30 minutes

3) Microscopy
- Fill the wells of microscopy chambers with 300 μL BSA (1 mg/mL) and wait for 1 hours for complete coating
- Wash with distilled/sterilized water 5-10 times
- Add 150 μL of GUV suspension to the wells
- Add 200 μL of PBS onto GUV suspension in the wells and mix the solution gently.
- Using Wide-Field Microscopy, set the magnification to 40x, excitation to 530 nm (green light), emission to 570 nm (yellow light)
