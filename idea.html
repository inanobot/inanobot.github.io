Janus motor

Janus particles are nanoparticles whose surfaces have two or more distinct physical properties. We will combine magnetic and biochemical compatible parts.

Biotin - Streptavidin

Connects liposome and Janus motor. Very strong: Dissociation constant is app. 10^-15 M. Sustained for extremes of pH, temperature, organic solvents and other denaturing agents.

Functionalized Liposome

Liposome with transmembrane proteins implanted within the bilipid layer, with specificity for aptamer tagged targets. Intakes the target molecule on contact.

Step 1

The motor is moved in 3-dimensions within a microfluidic channel using a magnetic particle controller

Step 2

The motor and the liposome link with each other upon biotin/streptavidin binding

Step 3

The motor is moved, pulling the liposome into a pool of the selected and labeled small molecule of choice for uptake

Step 4

The motor and liposome are moved into a peptide solution, which causes the membrane to burst and release contents
